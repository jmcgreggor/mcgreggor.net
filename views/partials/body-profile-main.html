STRENGTHS


Starting out with CliftonStrengths (previously StrengthsFinder), this assessment which is based on Positive Psychology principles, helps people identify areas of personal strengths. With the knowledge of my own strengths I am able to maximize the results of my work by both focusing more on what comes naturally and further developing those talents. Within the assessment there are 34 total strengths which are collected within 4 domains; the top 5 strengths are the most dominant.



Input | Achiever | Intellection | Relator | Learner



Achievers are very results oriented. To achieve my goals, I collect a lot of information that can be used to make or support strategic decisions, I continuously reflect on those decisions, and establish strong relationships with team members and business partners.



Developer | Maximizer



The Maximizer and Developer strengths, found in my top 10, are strengths that I am looking to focus more on over the next several years. These strengths mirror my goal to help others accomplish great things by finding ways to help individuals develop their own strengths while helping teams maximize on those strengths.


BEHAVIOR


While some assessments focus on personality or talents, DiSC asses your behavioral differences. What I find highly valuable with DiSC is that it provides insight into how I present myself to others while knowing why others may respond differently than what I would anticipate.



Dominance | Conscientiousess



As a person with the DC profile, I tend to place emphasis on achieving results that are high in quality and accuracy.


TYPE


The Myers-Briggs assessment provides a fascinating report into the psychological preferences that one may have in how they perceive the world and how they may prefer to interact with it. For me, it provided a introspective confirmation of my own preferences and personality type.
